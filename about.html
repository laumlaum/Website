About me

Marco Lau,electronics, and code.

A quick summary of where I'm at, what I'm building, and why I'm doing it.

I'm a second-year Electrical & Electronics Engineering student at the University of Auckland. I'm early in the degree, but I haven't waited to start building - I'd rather learn a concept and then go test it on real hardware than leave it on paper.

I'm drawn to control systems, embedded firmware, and mechanical/PCB design because I think automation and robotics are about to have a major breakthrough in the global market, and I want to be building the skills now to be part of that industry when it does. At the same time, I genuinely enjoy the subject for its own sake - I like the theory as much as the hands-on side, and the satisfaction of watching the two actually meet in something that works.

My projects usually start as a rough idea, then move through CAD, wiring, firmware, testing, debugging, and a lot of iteration. I like practical builds because they force every part of the design to be tested - the mechanical structure, the circuit, the code, and the way it's all assembled.

Current focus

  • Rotary inverted pendulum control
  • Stepper motor drivers
  • PCB layout and hardware testing
  • Clean documentation of builds
