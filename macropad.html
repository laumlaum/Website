Custom Macropad

A 3x3 mechanical macropad built as a first hands-on project in electronics, soldering, 3D printing, and firmware. It is now used to automate daily tasks on my PC.

Electronics Soldering Firmware USB HID 3D Printing
Overview

I wanted to start with a simple project that would introduce me to electronics, 3D printing, and soldering. The result was a custom 3x3 mechanical macropad with some mechanical switches and a 3D printed PLA enclosure.

The macropad connects to my PC and is now used to automate daily tasks, shortcuts, and repeated actions.

Problem

I wanted a small project that was simple enough to finish, but still covered real hardware skills. The goal was to build something useful while learning how switches, wiring, solder joints, firmware, and a physical enclosure all come together in one device.

Approach

The project was built over about a week, starting from the basic idea of a small shortcut keypad and developing it into a working 3x3 macropad.

Mechanical / Enclosure

The enclosure was 3D printed in PLA. It was designed to hold the switches securely while keeping the overall shape simple and compact. This gave me practice with part fitment, tolerances, and designing around real components.

Electronics

The macropad uses nine mechanical switches arranged in a 3x3 layout. I used Outemu Orange switches and soldered the electrical connections by hand. This helped me learn how to plan wiring, make reliable solder joints, and debug simple switch circuits.

Firmware

The firmware reads the switch inputs and sends keyboard shortcuts to the PC. This allowed the macropad to act as a USB input device for automating common actions and repeated tasks.

Result

The final result was a working 3x3 macropad that I now use on my PC for daily shortcuts and task automation. It gave me a practical introduction to electronics, soldering, firmware, and 3D printed enclosure design.

What's next

I am still deciding what to improve next. Possible upgrades include a cleaner enclosure design, custom keycaps, RGB lighting, a PCB version, or a more advanced firmware setup with multiple layers and profiles.
